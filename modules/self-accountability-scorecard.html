Five Accountability Pillars
🌟
Customer Experience (CX)
Client-first decisions. Clarity over technical elegance. Measured by: client articulation of value, CPE score trend, validation question completion rate.
💬
Clarity
Three Tests compliance rate. Clarification request frequency. Communication quality measured by client action rate, not communication volume.
👤
Ownership
Outcome Owner assignment rate. Issue orphan count. Runbook adherence. Measured by: named OO on every engagement, zero orphaned issues.
📡
Signal
Signal language usage rate. Signal stream monitoring consistency. Measured by: % of updates that qualify as signal vs. status, weekly review attendance.
🎯
Accountability
CEES Session attendance. Playbook contribution rate. Quarterly narrative submission. Measured by: cadence adherence, evidence documentation rate.
Drift Watch — Identifying Quiet Regressions

Drift Watch is the practice of identifying score declines before they become visible failures. A single week of lower performance is noise. Three consecutive weeks of decline is a drift signal that requires active intervention.

🔍 What Drift Looks Like
No single dramatic failure. Instead: Three Tests compliance drops from 94% to 87% over three weeks. Signal language usage declines from 82% to 68% over four weeks. Each week looks "fine" in isolation. The trend is the problem.
🚨 Drift Watch Triggers
Any pillar declining for 3+ consecutive weeks · Evidence rate below 80% for 2+ weeks · CEES Session attendance below 100% for 2 consecutive cycles · Three Tests compliance below 90% for any week
✅ Drift Response
Name the drift in the next Signal Review. Identify the specific behavior that changed. Design one concrete corrective action. Measure for two weeks. Document in quarterly narrative.

Quarterly Narrative — Format
1
Where the Standard Held
Identify 2–3 specific instances where you applied the EEP standard under pressure and it held. Name the engagement, the situation, and the behavior. Evidence required.
2
Where the Standard Slipped
Identify 1–2 specific instances where your behavior fell below the standard. Name the engagement, the situation, and what you did instead of the standard. No blame externalization.
3
What Changes Next Cycle
One specific, measurable behavioral change for the next 30 days. Not "I will communicate better." Instead: "I will apply all Three Tests to every client-facing email before sending, and log the review in ESXP."
4
Drift Watch Items
Name any pillar that declined for 2+ consecutive weeks. State the specific behavior that changed. State the corrective action and the measurement method for the next cycle.
