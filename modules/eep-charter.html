What the Charter Establishes

This Charter constitutes the formal organizational commitment document establishing the EEP as an operational standard. From the date of ratification, the EEP ceases to be an initiative, pilot, or enhancement effort and becomes the expected and enforced standard of delivery excellence for all client-facing engagements. It is not a collection of best practices assembled after the fact. It is an engineered system.

"Trustworthy by design, not by default. Client trust is not a byproduct of technical quality. It is a distinct output, with its own drivers, its own failure modes, and its own standards."
— EEP Charter, Program Lead: Mike Garcia
Five Non-Negotiable Commitments
Non-Negotiable I
Outcome Ownership
Every engagement has a named Outcome Owner, visible to the client in writing, before delivery begins. No engagement may proceed to active delivery without this designation in place.
✓ Compliance: OO named, communicated in writing, before Day One.
✗ Violation: Delivery begins without written OO confirmation to client.
Non-Negotiable II
Communication Standards
Every client-facing communication passes the Three Communication Tests before it is sent — no exceptions. Communications that do not pass all three tests are not sent. There is no expedience exception.
✓ Compliance: All three tests applied to every client-facing communication before sending.
✗ Violation: Any client communication sent without Three Tests review.
Non-Negotiable III
Value Validation at Close
Every engagement closes with validated client articulation of value received. No engagement is administratively closed without this record in the engagement file. Task completion is not closure.
✓ Compliance: Three validation questions asked, client articulation documented before close.
✗ Violation: Engagement closed on milestone sign-off without client value articulation.
Non-Negotiable IV
Runbook Adherence
Every delivery incident follows the applicable Runbook — Escalation, Communication, Handoff, or Incident Response — without improvisation in high-pressure scenarios. The Runbook is the judgment.
✓ Compliance: Applicable runbook followed for every delivery incident, no improvisation.
✗ Violation: Incident handled by improvisation rather than runbook protocol.
Non-Negotiable V
Operating Cadences
The four operating cadences are non-negotiable standing commitments, not optional enhancements. They are not to be cancelled, deferred, or abbreviated due to schedule pressure or workload.
✓ Compliance: All four cadences held on schedule every cycle.
✗ Violation: Any cadence cancelled, deferred, or abbreviated without documented cause.
