What EDEv4 Introduces
Three Principles With No Equivalent in ITIL, PMI, or SAFe
These three principles are structurally new — not refinements of existing concepts, but genuinely different approaches to the problem of client trust.
🎯
Principle 1
Trust as a Distinct, Engineered Output
ITIL, PMI, and SAFe treat client trust as a byproduct of good delivery. EDEv4 treats it as a distinct output with its own drivers, failure modes, and production process.

This means trust is measured separately from delivery quality, trust failures can occur even when delivery succeeds, and trust-building behaviors are specified as non-negotiable standards.

"Trustworthy by design, not by default."
📡
Principle 2
Signal Intelligence as a Predictive Discipline
Monitoring client relationship health through observable behavioral signals — on a weekly cadence, with specific flag conditions — has no equivalent in any established delivery framework.

These frameworks measure what happened. Signal Intelligence measures what is about to happen. The weekly cadence is the critical design choice: fast enough to catch leading indicators 4–8 weeks before formal escalation.

Gartner (2024): 4.2× earlier detection.
👤
Principle 3
Outcome Ownership as a Trust Architecture
One named person owns the client's experience — communicated to the client in writing before delivery begins. Structurally different from RACI matrices and distributed team accountability.

Those models answer "who does what?" Outcome Ownership answers "who does the client trust?" Trust is personal, not organizational. Clients trust people, not processes.

McKinsey (2023): 72% of failures from unclear ownership.

Integration Model
How EDEv4 Works With Existing Frameworks
EDEv4 does not replace ITIL, PMI, or SAFe. It adds the trust layer they leave open. Teams running any of these frameworks can adopt EDEv4 without replacing their existing methodology.
EDEv4 + ITIL 4
ITIL governs service reliability. EDEv4 governs the client's experience of that reliability. The Signal Intelligence streams complement ITIL's incident management by detecting client-visible friction before it becomes a formal incident. A team can achieve 100% SLA compliance while the client experiences the engagement as chaotic and unaccountable.
ITIL: Service reliability · EDEv4: Client trust engineering
EDEv4 + PMI / PMBOK
PMI governs project delivery within scope, schedule, and budget. EDEv4 governs what the client experiences across that delivery arc. The Outcome Owner model complements the PM role by adding client-visible accountability. The Three Tests complement the Communications Management Plan by adding a quality standard for individual communications.
PMI: Project delivery · EDEv4: Client experience layer
EDEv4 + SAFe
SAFe governs software delivery velocity and team alignment. EDEv4 governs the client's experience of that velocity. The CEES Session complements the SAFe retrospective by adding a standard-based learning mechanism. The Outcome Validation layer complements the SAFe DoD by adding a client-perspective closure standard.
SAFe: Software velocity · EDEv4: Trust architecture
ITIL, PMI, and SAFe are delivery frameworks.
EDEv4 is a trust framework.

Delivery frameworks answer: "How do we produce the work reliably?"
EDEv4 answers: "How do we ensure the client experiences the work as valuable, and trusts us enough to renew, reference, and expand?"
