The Partnership Model

The CSAM owns the client relationship. The EDE owns the delivery outcome. These are distinct accountabilities — and the partnership between them is what determines whether a technically excellent engagement also produces a referenceable client. A CSAM who is surprised by a delivery problem loses trust with the client. An EDE who operates without CSAM alignment loses routing priority. The partnership rhythm is not optional — it is the mechanism that converts individual delivery quality into organizational trust.

"They own the relationship; you own the outcome. Make the CSAM look prepared in every client interaction — no surprises, ever."
— EEP CSAM Partnership Standard
Five Partnership Principles
1
Make the CSAM Look Prepared — No Surprises
Every client-facing moment must be coordinated with the CSAM before it happens. If the CSAM is surprised by a delivery problem, a client escalation, or a scope change — the partnership has failed. Pre-brief before every client interaction above routine status. The CSAM should never learn about a problem from the client.
2
Signal-Over-Status Updates
Updates to the CSAM must communicate what changes decisions — not just what happened. "The call went well" is not an update. "Customer Friction is amber — the client re-asked the same question twice this week, which suggests our last communication didn't land clearly" is signal. The CSAM needs to know what to do differently, not just what occurred.
3
Disagree in Private, Align in Public
Disagreements between EDE and CSAM are resolved before the client interaction — never in front of the client. A unified front is non-negotiable. If you disagree with the CSAM's approach, raise it in the weekly check-in. In the client call, you are one team with one position. Visible disagreement between EDE and CSAM destroys client confidence immediately.
4
Outcome Owner Call — Shared Between EDE and CSAM
The Outcome Owner call is a joint accountability moment. Both EDE and CSAM are present. The client sees a unified team with a single named owner. This is the primary trust-building cadence in the partnership model — it demonstrates that the relationship owner and the delivery owner are coordinated and accountable together.
5
Weekly CSAM Check-In — Non-Negotiable
The weekly check-in is not a status call — it is a signal-sharing session. What friction indicators did you observe this week? What communications are going out? What does the CSAM need to know before their next client interaction? This cadence is a Non-Negotiable Commitment under the EEP Charter. It cannot be cancelled, deferred, or abbreviated.
Surprise Index — Three Levels
✅ Zero Surprises — Target State
CSAM is pre-briefed before every client interaction. No unplanned escalations. No client-initiated surprises. CSAM can answer any client question about delivery status without contacting the EDE first. This is the standard.
⚠️ Amber — One Unplanned Event
One unplanned escalation or client-initiated surprise this month. CSAM was not fully prepared for one client interaction. Requires immediate debrief and process correction. Two consecutive amber months triggers a partnership review.
🔴 Red — Routing Trust at Risk
Client raised a concern directly to CSAM without prior EDE notification. CSAM was caught unprepared in a client interaction. Routing trust score below threshold. Immediate escalation and partnership reset required.
Routing Trust — How It's Built and Eroded
✅ Trust-Building Behaviors
Consistent pre-briefing before client interactions · Signal-quality updates that change CSAM decisions · Zero surprises over multiple engagements · Outcome Owner calls held on schedule · CSAM can predict EDE behavior under pressure
❌ Trust-Eroding Behaviors
CSAM learns about problems from the client · Status updates that don't change decisions · Missed weekly check-ins · Visible disagreement in client interactions · Outcome Owner call cancelled or deferred
"Routing trust is built over multiple engagements and eroded in a single interaction. The CSAM routes future work to engineers they can predict."
