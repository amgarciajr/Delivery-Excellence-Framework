Core Operating Model

Customer Engineer Excellence Standard

The CEES Playbook strips away reliance on specific internal tools and defines the behavioral standard for technical delivery. It is the framework for operating as a Trusted Advisor rather than a reactive order-taker.

01 / The Identity Shift

Technical brilliance is the baseline. Our differentiation is how we manage customer anxiety and business risk. Engineers operating under CEES must transition their identity:

  • ✕ From Order Taker: Waits for a ticket, executes the technical requirement exactly as written, and closes the loop silently.
  • ✓ To Trusted Advisor: Questions the requirement to understand the business outcome, names the risks before they happen, and establishes explicit communication cadence.

02 / Agnostic Delivery Mechanics

The Three Tests

All outbound communications (regardless of the ticketing system used) must pass this validation:

  • Repeat-Back: Can the business stakeholder repeat this update to their leadership without technical jargon?
  • Stakes: Are the consequences of inaction clearly stated?
  • Next-Step: Is there a specific owner and exact time for the next touchpoint?

Account Partner Alignment

We do not operate in silos. The engineer is responsible for pushing qualitative signals upstream to the Account Executive / Client Partner.

  • Is the customer's technical team adopting the solution?
  • Is there shadow friction the dashboard isn't catching?
  • Are there unaddressed skills gaps threatening the deployment?

03 / The Communication SLA

Severity Level Initial Response Update Cadence Outcome Owner Focus
Critical (Sev-A) 15 Minutes Hourly (Even if no new data) Stabilize first, Diagnose second. Shield the technical fixers from customer pinging.
High (Sev-B) 2 Hours Daily (EOD minimum) Determine business impact. Provide clear workarounds while fix is developed.
Standard (Sev-C) 24 Hours Every 48-72 Hours Execute Three-Tests communication. Drive the project timeline proactively.
