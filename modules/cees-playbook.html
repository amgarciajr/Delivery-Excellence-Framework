What CEEDS Is

CEEDS is the team-level operating framework of the EEP. Where EDEv4 governs what happens at the engagement level, CEEDS governs how the team operates day-to-day as a cultural and organizational unit. The four pillars define the team's values in behavioral terms. The five operating motions define the team's recurring commitments. The runbooks define pre-decided responses to high-stakes situations.

"Teams that sustain the CEES Session cadence demonstrate measurably faster judgment convergence and lower variance in client experience outcomes than teams that allow the cadence to slip."
— CEEDS Research Finding
The Four CEEDS Pillars
Pillar 01
Customer Experience Over Technical Perfection
When a choice must be made between technically elegant but confusing vs. technically sufficient but clearly understood — choose clarity. The measure of delivery quality is the client's ability to act on what was delivered. A technically perfect solution the client cannot use has failed.
❌ Violation: Delivering technically correct work the client cannot explain or act on.
Pillar 02
Clarity Over Complexity
The measure of a communication is not sophistication but comprehension. The standard is always the client's ability to act. If the client cannot restate what you communicated, the communication failed — regardless of technical accuracy. Simplify until the client can act, then stop.
❌ Violation: Communications that require follow-up clarification from the client.
Pillar 03
Ownership Without Boundaries
An EEP team member does not leave an issue unaddressed because it falls outside their defined role. Ownership is volunteered, not assigned by default. If you see a problem affecting the client's outcome — regardless of whether it's "your" problem — you own it until resolved or explicitly handed off.
❌ Violation: "That's not my scope" when a client issue goes unaddressed.
Pillar 04
Signal Over Opinion
Engagement health is assessed through observable, tracked indicators — not individual intuition or seniority-weighted opinion. "I think the client is happy" is not signal. "Customer Friction is green — no repeat questions in two weeks" is signal. The signal framework removes subjective assessment from engagement health evaluation.
❌ Violation: Reporting engagement health based on gut feel rather than tracked signal streams.
Five Operating Motions
📡
Weekly Customer Signal Review
Every week · Program Lead / Engagement Lead
Review all three signal streams. Surface amber and red flags. Assign owners to every flagged item. Output: engagement health status with named actions.
🚀
Ownership Kickoff
Per engagement start · Engagement Lead
Named Outcome Owner documented and communicated to client in writing before delivery begins. Non-Negotiable I. No engagement proceeds without this.
👤
Per-Issue Owner Assignment
Per new issue · Engagement Lead
Every open issue has a named owner and committed resolution date. No orphaned issues. Client can always answer "who owns this?" without asking.
🎯
CEES Simulation Session
Bi-weekly · Program Lead / Certified Trainer
Structured scenario-based practice. Team works through real engagement dilemmas. Judgment calibrated against team standard. Scenario library updated with real cases.
🔄
Signal Framework Review
Quarterly · Program Lead
Signal indicators updated to reflect current portfolio. Playbook revised based on real signal data. Program evolution documented.
Four Runbooks
"The Runbook is the judgment. Pre-decided frameworks remove improvisation from high-stakes moments — when pressure is highest and judgment is most likely to fail."
🚨 Escalation Runbook
Defines exactly how an issue is escalated: who is notified, in what sequence, with what information, and on what timeline. No improvisation. No "I'll figure it out as I go." The runbook is the decision.
✉️ Communication Runbook
Applies the Three Tests to client communications. Defines who approves client-facing messages above a defined sensitivity threshold. Every high-stakes communication follows this runbook before sending.
🔁 Handoff Runbook
Governs how engagement ownership transitions between team members or project phases without losing the client's accumulated trust context. Silent handoffs are a Non-Negotiable violation.
🛠️ Incident Response Runbook
Defines the structured response to delivery incidents carrying client relationship risk — including communication sequence, Outcome Owner assignment, and post-incident review process.
CEES Session Format — How a Session Runs
1
Setup Narrative (5 min)
Facilitator presents a specific, detailed scenario from real delivery. Context, triggering event, and team's observable behavior described. No vague generalities — specific enough to reconstruct.
2
Reconstruction Questions (10 min)
Fact-seeking questions that build progressively from general context to specific behavior. "What specific words did the client use?" Not "What do you think went wrong?"
3
Standard Contrast (10 min)
Guide participants through comparison of described behavior against the WGLL standard. "Looking at the Communication Clarity pillar — what would above-standard behavior have looked like?"
4
Lesson Statement (5 min)
Single declarative statement of the learning the scenario produces. Specific and transferable. "When a client uses qualified language in a concern, the signal is the qualification, not the certainty."
5
Next Practice Rep (5 min)
Specific prompt directing the team to a concrete, near-term opportunity to practice the behavior identified in the lesson. Closes the loop from scenario to action.
