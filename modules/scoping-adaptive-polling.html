Why Pre-Engagement Determines Everything

The six steps before Day One are not administrative overhead — they are the primary determinant of CPE outcomes. Engineers who complete all six steps consistently outperform those who skip or abbreviate them, regardless of technical skill level. The scoping call discovers the real business problem. The recap email creates written alignment. The pre-poll captures expectations before delivery shapes them. The live poll enables real-time course correction. Together, they transform delivery from a guess into a calibrated response.

"The six steps before Day One determine most CPE outcomes. An engineer who arrives prepared, aligned, and polling in real time is not doing more work — they are doing the right work."
— EDEv4 Pre-Engagement Standard
The Six Pre-Delivery Steps — In Sequence
1
Scoping Call — Discover the Real Business Problem
The scoping call must happen 2–4 weeks before delivery. Its purpose is not to confirm logistics — it is to discover the real business problem behind the engagement request. What does the client actually need? What does success look like to them? What constraints exist that the dispatch description doesn't capture? Never book travel before CSAM and manager confirm.

2
Recap Email — Written Alignment Before Delivery
The recap email converts the scoping call into a written record of alignment. It confirms scope, confirms the Outcome Owner, and gives the client an opportunity to correct misunderstandings before delivery begins. Apply all Three Tests before sending. Tag with #Scoping. Use TPL-SCOP template.

3
Pre-Poll — Expectations, Expertise, Objectives
The pre-poll is sent before Day One to capture the audience's expectations, expertise level, and specific objectives. It is not a survey — it is intelligence gathering that directly shapes delivery design. Send with #AdaptivePolling tag. Apply Three Tests. Use TPL-POLL template. Results must be reviewed before delivery begins.

4
Workshop Materials Upload — Early, Not Late
Materials must be uploaded on time. Late uploads are flagged amber even if eventually completed — the on-time flag matters independently. Confirm the bill of materials. Verify lab environment via aka.ms/LOD. Late uploads signal poor operational hygiene regardless of content quality.

5
Technical Contact Confirmed
The correct technical contact — the customer contact you will interact with most — must be entered on the dispatch and documented in the engagement record. This is not the project sponsor or the CSAM. It is the person who will be in the room, asking questions, and whose experience determines the CPE score.
Documented in ESXPNot the sponsor

Live Poll — Adapt in Real Time
The live poll is run during delivery to capture real-time feedback and enable course correction. It is not a formality — it is the mechanism that converts pre-engagement intelligence into in-delivery adaptation. Results are captured and inform the post-engagement learning loop. No exceptions for short engagements.

Adaptive Polling — Three Types
📋 Pre-Poll (Before Day One)
Sent 1–2 weeks before delivery. Captures expectations, expertise level, and specific objectives. Shapes delivery design before the first session begins. Required for all engagements — no exceptions.
📡 Live Poll (During Delivery)
Run during delivery sessions to capture real-time feedback. Enables course correction mid-engagement. Results documented and fed into the post-engagement learning loop. The mechanism that makes delivery adaptive rather than scripted.
📞 Follow-Up Poll (Two Weeks Post)
The two-week follow-up contact using the three validation questions. Closes the CPE loop. Captures client articulation of value received. Required for clean engagement closure under EEP standard.
Exceptions — Only Two Apply
"Dispatch type changes entry, not the standard. Short engagements still require polling. The only exceptions are SCOP and Lab Assist."
✅ SCOP — Scoping-Only Engagement
A scoping-only engagement is itself the pre-engagement step. The scoping call IS the delivery. Pre-poll and live poll requirements are modified — but the recap email and written alignment are still required.
✅ Lab Assist
Lab Assist engagements have a modified polling requirement due to the nature of the delivery format. However, the Outcome Owner assignment, CSAM contact, and recap email requirements remain in full effect.
❌ "It's a short engagement"
Not an exception. A one-day engagement has the same polling standard as a five-day engagement. The standard scales in application, not in applicability. Short engagements that skip polling consistently produce lower CPE scores.
Applying the Three Tests — Recap Email Example
TestQuestionPass Criteria for Recap Email
Repeat-Back Test Could the client accurately restate the key content after one reading? Scope is stated in plain language. Outcome Owner is named. Delivery dates are explicit. No jargon that requires interpretation.
Stakes Test Does the client understand what happens if they don't respond or correct? Email states that delivery will proceed based on this scope unless the client responds with corrections by [date]. Consequence of non-response is explicit.
Next-Step Test Is the immediate next action crystal clear — who, when, how confirmed? "Please reply to confirm this scope is accurate by [date]. If no response is received, we will proceed as described." One action. One owner. One deadline.
