The Core Principle — Entry Changes, Standard Doesn't

Quick Match and self-dispatch are different entry points into the delivery queue — not different delivery standards. An engineer who receives a Quick Match dispatch has less pre-engagement lead time than one who receives a proactive dispatch. That changes the timeline for completing the six pre-delivery steps. It does not change the requirement to complete them. The standard is the standard regardless of how the engagement arrived.

"Dispatch type changes entry, not the standard. A Quick Match engagement that skips polling is not an efficient delivery — it is a non-compliant one."
— EDEv4 Dispatch Standard
Four Dispatch Types — Taxonomy
Type 01 — Proactive
Proactive Dispatch
Standard pre-engagement lead time. Full six-step ValueAdd Chain applies with normal timing. CSAM-initiated. Scoping call 2–4 weeks pre-delivery. All pre-delivery steps completed before Day One.

Quick Match
Compressed lead time. All six pre-delivery steps still required — compressed into the available window. First 48 hours are critical: CSAM contact, scoping, recap, pre-poll must all be initiated immediately. Weekly Quick Match check required to maintain queue discipline.

Type 03 — RMOT
RMOT (Shared Pool)
Multiple engineers in a shared delivery pool. Single Outcome Owner assigned per engagement regardless of pool size. Shared ownership is no ownership — one name, communicated to the client in writing. RMOT does not modify the polling or communication standards.
Shared poolSingle OO requiredStandard applies
Type 04 — Self-Dispatch
Self-Dispatch
Engineer-initiated engagement. Same standard applies — no exceptions for self-initiated work. The fact that you chose the engagement does not reduce the pre-delivery requirements. All six steps, full polling, Outcome Owner assignment in writing before delivery begins.

First 48 Hours — Required Actions
  • 📞
    CSAM Contact
    Notify CSAM within 48hrs of dispatch acceptance. Confirm they are aware of the engagement and aligned on the approach. Never proceed without CSAM awareness.
  • 🎯
    Scoping Call Scheduled
    Schedule the scoping call immediately — even if compressed. Discover the real business problem before any delivery planning begins. Use TPL-SCOP.
  • 📧
    Recap Email Sent
    Written alignment before delivery. Apply Three Tests. Tag #Scoping. Confirm scope, Outcome Owner, and delivery dates in writing.
  • 📋
    Pre-Poll Deployed
    Send pre-poll immediately — even for short engagements. Tag #AdaptivePolling. Apply Three Tests. Review results before Day One.
  • 👤
    Outcome Owner Assigned in Writing
    Named, communicated to client before delivery begins. Non-Negotiable I. No engagement proceeds without this documented.
RMOT Pool — Single Outcome Owner Rule
"Shared ownership is no ownership. In an RMOT pool of four engineers, exactly one is the Outcome Owner — named, communicated to the client in writing, accountable for the outcome."
✅ Correct RMOT Structure
Pool of 3 engineers. One named Outcome Owner communicated to client before delivery. Other engineers deliver under the OO's accountability. Client knows exactly who owns the outcome.
❌ RMOT Anti-Pattern
"We're all responsible" — this is the most common RMOT failure. When everyone owns it, no one owns it. The client cannot answer "who owns this?" and trust erodes immediately.
📋 Weekly RMOT Check
Review all RMOT engagements weekly. Confirm OO assignment is current. Confirm client has OO name in writing. Confirm no orphaned issues in the pool.
