What Is Operational Hygiene?

Operational Hygiene is the discipline of making your delivery visible, predictable, and accountable — before, during, and after every engagement. It represents the 40–45% of bonus eligibility that is entirely within the engineer's control. No client dependency. No external variable. Just disciplined execution of the standard.

"No silent work. No late surprises. Every commitment tracked, every update signal-quality, every engagement owned by name."
— EEP Operational Standard
Six Core Concepts
  • ✅
    Dispatch Acceptance — A Commitment, Not a Placeholder
    When you accept a dispatch, you are making a binding commitment to the client, the CSAM, and the program. Acceptance is not a queue position — it is a promise. Every accepted dispatch must have a named Outcome Owner assigned before delivery begins.
  • 📋
    ESXP Logging — If It's Not Logged, It Didn't Happen
    Every engagement action, status update, and client interaction must be logged in ESXP. Unlogged work is invisible work. Invisible work cannot be measured, defended, or improved. The log is the record of delivery — not your memory.
  • 📡
    Status Cadence — Signal Over Status
    Updates must change decisions, not just report activity. "Things are going well" is status. "Customer Friction is amber — the client re-asked the same question twice this week" is signal. Every update you send should answer: what does the recipient need to do differently because of this message?
  • 🔗
    ValueAdd Chain — Six Steps Before Day One
    The six pre-delivery steps (scoping call, recap email, pre-poll, material upload, technical contact confirmation, outcome owner assignment) determine the majority of CPE outcomes. Skipping any step is not efficiency — it is risk transfer to the client relationship.
  • 📊
    CPE Workflow — Survey Window, Follow-Up, Learning Loop
    The CPE process has three phases: the survey window (active monitoring and follow-up to maximize response rate), the follow-up (two-week post-delivery contact using the three validation questions), and the learning loop (what did this engagement teach us that changes how we deliver next time?).
  • 👤
    Outcome Owner — One Named Owner Per Engagement
    Every engagement has exactly one named Outcome Owner, communicated to the client in writing before delivery begins. Every open issue has one named owner. Every escalation has one named owner. Shared ownership is no ownership. The client must be able to answer "who owns this?" without ambiguity.
ValueAdd Chain — Six Steps
1
Scoping Call (2–4 weeks pre-delivery)
Discover the real business problem. Confirm scope in writing. Use TPL-SCOP template. Never book travel before CSAM/manager confirms.
2
Pre-Engagement Email + Pre-Poll
Send #AdaptivePolling tagged email. Apply Three Tests. Capture expectations, expertise, and objectives before Day One.
3
Workshop Materials Upload (Early)
Upload on-time — late uploads flagged amber even if completed. Bill of materials confirmed. Lab environment via aka.ms/LOD verified.
4
Technical Contact Confirmed
Correct technical contact entered on dispatch. The customer contact you interact with most — documented in engagement record.
5
Outcome Owner Assigned in Writing
Named, communicated to client before delivery begins. Non-Negotiable I of the EEP Charter. No engagement proceeds without this.
6
Adaptive Polling Executed
Pre-poll sent, live poll run, results captured. Adapt delivery in real time based on poll data. No exceptions for short engagements.
CPE Workflow — Three Phases
📋 Phase 1 — Survey Window
Active monitoring of survey completion. Follow up with non-respondents. The survey window is a delivery activity, not an administrative one. Your CPE score is determined here.
📞 Phase 2 — Two-Week Follow-Up
Contact the client two weeks post-delivery. Ask the three validation questions: What did we deliver that you didn't have before? How has that changed what you can do? What would you tell a peer?
🔄 Phase 3 — Learning Loop
What did this engagement teach us? What signal did we miss? What would we do differently? Document in ESXP. Contribute to CEES Session scenario library if applicable.
"An engagement that closes with a signed milestone document but no client articulation of value has not closed cleanly under the EEP standard."
Signal Language vs. Status Language
❌ Status: "Things are going well"
No decision changes. No action required. Invisible to the program. Does not meet EEP communication standard.
⚠ Status: "No issues to report"
Absence of signal is not signal. This update tells the reader nothing actionable. Fails the Stakes Test and Next-Step Test.
✅ Signal: "Ownership Follow-Through is amber — two open items past committed date"
Observable. Specific. Actionable. Changes what the reader does next. This is the EEP communication standard.
